Introduction

This analysis focuses on building violations in Chicago's 15th Ward, using a dataset with 784,225 observations and 22 variables. The study aims to uncover trends and insights into regulatory compliance.

Key Steps and Findings

Data Import and Cleaning

  • Imported data with 784,225 rows and 22 columns.
  • Addressed missing values (initially 0.27% missing, increased to 2.77% after converting blanks to NA).
  • Converted date variables to POSIXct format.
  • Created new variables:
    • CityDelay: Days between hearing and violation dates.
    • Season: Categorized violations into Winter, Spring, Summer, and Fall.

Data Analysis

  • 452 unique violation descriptions and 456 unique codes.
  • "AVE" was the most common street type.
  • Weak positive correlation (0.2242) between fines and administrative costs.
  • Average fines have declined over time, with 2024 showing the lowest due to an increase in "Continuance" cases.

Insights and Observations

  • "Liable" was the most common case disposition, while "Default" incurred the highest fines.
  • Violations peaked in summer, possibly due to increased construction activity.
  • Many offenders opted to pay fines directly, leading to prevalent default judgments.

Interesting Facts About the 15th Ward

  • Includes Brighton Park, Gage Park, and West Englewood neighborhoods.
  • The first major manufacturing industry in Chicago began here in 1857.
  • Current Alderman: Raymond A. Lopez.

Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates the value of data mining for real-world applications. Through statistical and geospatial exploration, the study highlights patterns in violations, fines, and compliance in the 15th Ward. These insights provide a foundation for informed decision-making and improved regulatory enforcement.
