Credit Card Dataset Analysis: Factors Influencing Income

The dataset captures financial and demographic attributes of individuals. Key variables include dependents, monthly credit card expenditure, ownership status, self-employment, and the number of major credit cards.

Methodology

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

A moderate positive correlation (r=0.318) was observed between income and dependents. Linear regression revealed:

Income = 2.917 + 0.445 × Dependents

This suggests that income tends to rise moderately with an increase in dependents.

Regression Model Development

A multivariate regression model included significant predictors such as expenditure, ownership status, self-employment, dependents, and major cards. The model had an adjusted R² = 0.2581, explaining 26% of income variability.

Model Evaluation

Training Set: RMSE = 1.647, MAE = 1.144

Validation Set: RMSE = 1.541, MAE = 1.111

The model showed good generalization with no overfitting.

Feature Engineering

Highly correlated variables like "share" were removed, and categorical variables (e.g., owner and card) were encoded as dummy variables.

Results and Key Insights

  • Dependents: A significant predictor of income but explains only part of its variability.
  • Other Factors: Expenditure, ownership status, and self-employment also play critical roles.
  • Additional Influences: Other unaccounted factors like education and geographic location may further impact income.
