Key Insights

  • Dominant Lower-Priced Properties: Most properties are under $2M.
  • Growing Mid-Tier Market: $2M-$4M residential segment expanding.
  • Limited High-End Properties: Scarcity of properties above $4M, indicating niche demand.

Demographic Conclusion

  • Market caters to affordable and mid-range buyers, with a growing middle-tier market.
  • Commercial spaces remain accessible, and luxury properties target a niche, exclusive demographic.
  • Likely median age of residents: 35-45 years, including young professionals and families.

Final Conclusion

  • Residential Market Evolution: The demand for mid-tier properties ($2M-$4M) has grown, while affordable properties below $2M continue to dominate the market. High-end properties above $4M remain rare, signaling a niche demand.
  • Commercial Market Stability: The commercial market showed steady growth in 2009-2013, with consistent demand from 2014-2020, especially in prime locations. This indicates Tribeca's attractiveness for small-to-mid businesses.
  • Price Trends: Mid-tier properties have seen rising demand, while the luxury segment has remained scarce, suggesting limited supply and targeted demand from high-net-worth individuals.
  • Market Composition: Tribeca continues to offer a diverse range of properties, with the residential market catering to a broad buyer spectrum, while the commercial market supports small-to-mid businesses. The luxury segment remains exclusive and caters to a select group of investors.
